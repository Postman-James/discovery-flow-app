Frame the Call

0-3 min

Set the tone. You're here to listen, not pitch. Get permission to ask questions.

"Thanks for taking the time. I'd love to understand how your team builds and manages APIs today so we can figure out if there's a fit. I'll mostly be asking questions -- sound good?" click to copy

Checklist

  • Confirm how much time they have
  • Set expectation: "I'll be asking questions to understand your world"
  • Confirm attendees and their roles
  • Ask if there's anything specific they want to cover

Notes

Start Broad

3-8 min

Understand who they are, what success looks like, and why APIs matter in their world.

Role and Context

  • "Can you walk me through your role and the teams you work most closely with?"
  • "Are you more hands-on, coordination-focused, or setting strategy?"

Success Metrics

  • "What are the top outcomes your team is measured on this quarter or year?"
  • "When leadership asks for API progress, what metrics do they care about most?"

Strategic Context

  • "What are the priorities or strategic initiatives going on in your company?"
  • "How does API fit into or support your company-wide business goals?"
  • "Is your organization doing anything to formalize API collaboration -- a platform initiative, partner program, or developer portal?"

Day-to-Day

  • "What does a typical week look like for you when it comes to APIs -- where do most of your hours go?"
  • "What parts of the process feel smooth today, and where does it usually slow down?"

Notes

Identify the Chaos Type

8-10 min

Use the 5 anchor questions to identify which chaos type they have. Select one or more below based on what you hear.

5 Anchor Questions

  • "How do teams discover internal APIs today?"
  • "When someone needs to integrate with another team's API, what does day one look like -- and what usually slows them down?"
  • "When an API ships or changes, how do docs, specs, tests, and what's actually running stay in sync -- or where do you see drift?"
  • "Are humans, agents, or both expected to consume these interfaces?"
  • "What would improve if finding, validating, and operating against those APIs got a lot easier?"

Select the Chaos Type(s) You're Hearing

🔍

Discovery / Reuse

Teams can't find, trust, or reuse internal APIs. "No single place", "people ask in Slack"

⚡

Execution / Quality

They find APIs but don't know if they work or who owns them. "Docs were wrong", "found out in prod"

🤖

Agent / Tool

Moving toward agents but no trusted interface layer. "No clean tool layer underneath"

🔀

Workflow Fragmentation

API truth scattered across code, docs, tests, CI, runtime. "Nobody sees it together"

Deep Dive Questions

10-22 min

Based on the chaos type(s) you identified, drill into the relevant questions. Questions are shown for your selected chaos types. Click any to mark as asked.

🔍 Discovery / Reuse

API Catalog
"How do developers discover the right internal API or service today?"
  • "If I joined a new team tomorrow, where would I look first?"
  • "Would I find approved APIs in one place, or piecing it together from repos, docs, Slack, tribal knowledge?"
  • "How often do teams build or request something that already exists?"

Signals

"It depends who you ask"
"We have Backstage but people still ask in Slack"
"There's no trusted source"
"We have a portal everyone uses" (weak pain)
"How do you know whether an API is actually ready for broader internal use?"
  • "Is there a readiness checklist?"
  • "Do you look at tests, examples, latency, pass rate, ownership, docs?"
  • "Who decides an API is ready to be published or recommended internally?"
"How are internal APIs or tools made available to non-owning teams?"
  • "Is there a formal publishing/distribution model?"
  • "Do teams know which APIs are approved versus experimental?"
  • "Do consumers get examples and runnable assets, or only docs?"

⚡ Execution / Quality

Health + CI/CD + Ownership
"Walk me through what happens when a developer needs to call an API owned by another team -- not one they built."
  • "What's the first thing they do -- Slack someone, open a portal, clone a repo?"
  • "If the first request fails, how do they figure out if it's their usage, the contract, or the service?"
  • "How long until they know who owns it?"
  • "Do they ever discover that docs or the spec were wrong after they've already built to them?"
"They usually have to ask the owning team"
"Ownership is stale" / "Docs were wrong"
"We found out in prod"
"Clear path, current artifacts" (weak pain)
"What usually breaks down between building an API and another team actually consuming it?"
  • "Is it discoverability, examples, test coverage, stale docs, auth, env config, ownership, or runtime surprises?"
  • "Where do handoffs fail most often?"
  • "What creates the most support burden for the API-owning team?"
"When something fails in production, how quickly can the consuming team determine whether it's the contract, the implementation, the environment, or their own usage?"
  • "Do they have one workflow for that, or jump across multiple tools?"
  • "Can they correlate test history with runtime behavior?"

Unbreakable APIs: Local Dev and CI/CD

"Are your devs able to run the exact same tests on their laptops that you're running in your CI/CD pipelines?"
  • "How much time do frontend and QA folks lose waiting for a backend service to be finished?"
  • "How do you keep tabs on pipeline performance, like which endpoints are failing most often?"

🔀 Workflow Fragmentation

Git-connected Catalog
"When an API changes in code, what else has to get updated -- OpenAPI, internal wiki, collections, consumer comms -- and what usually lags or never gets touched?"
  • "Have you seen the spec and prod behavior not match? How often?"
  • "If two engineers answered 'what does this endpoint do today?' -- one from the repo, one from staging -- would they always agree?"
  • "Is it mostly code-first, spec-first, or mixed?"
"Drift" / "Spec behind"
"Two different stories depending where you look"
"Stays aligned" / "One obvious place" (weak pain)

Unbreakable APIs: API Visibility

"Do you have a single system of record for API specs, ownership, and status?"
  • "How do you currently detect API breaking changes?"
  • "What happens operationally when a breaking change is identified?"
  • "Is there a centralized way to see which APIs are active, deprecated, or unmanaged?"

Unbreakable APIs: API Drift and Governance

"How do you make sure the code in your Git repo stays in sync with the docs and tests your devs use every day?"
  • "Is catching a breaking change a manual code review, or automated in the PR?"
  • "Do you usually find out an API has drifted through an internal alert, or a customer report?"

🤖 Agent / Tool

Agent Mode + MCP
"Are agents or AI workflows expected to call internal APIs or tools?"
  • "Do you need agents to discover tools safely?"
  • "How do you decide which tools are approved for agent use?"
  • "How do you test or validate what an agent can call?"
  • "Do you care more about the agent UX, or the trustworthiness of the interfaces underneath?"
"We care about agents but don't have a clean tool layer"
"Stay in Cursor but need reliable API context"
"No agent plans" (weak here)

Where Developers Work

"Where are your developers doing most of their work today -- IDE, GitHub, CI, Postman, Backstage, Datadog, Cursor?"
  • "Do they want to stay in Cursor / VS Code / terminal?"
  • "Would they reject another standalone AI layer?"
  • "Is the problem the interface, or the lack of trusted context underneath?"

Cross-Cutting Questions

Who Feels the Pain

  • "Which teams feel this pain most acutely today?" (Platform? App teams? Producers? Consumers? DX? AI builders?)

What They've Already Tried

  • "What have you already tried to solve this?" (Backstage, Datadog catalog, SwaggerHub, wiki, homegrown portal?)
  • "What does that current approach do well?"
  • "What still falls through the cracks?"
  • "What questions still require Slack or tribal knowledge?"

Close the Call

25-30 min

Lock down trigger, KPI, urgency, future state, constraints, decision dynamics. Check off each as you ask.

Trigger "What sparked this -- or what's on the calendar that makes API/platform visibility more urgent?"
KPI "If we fixed this, would leadership notice speed, risk, cost, or revenue first?"
Urgency "When do you need to show progress -- or is there an immovable date?"
Future State "Realistically -- what does 'good' look like here, in your words?"
Constraints "Anything that's a hard no for engineering or security we should never pitch?"
Decision Dynamics "Who besides you has to buy in -- budget vs technical?"
Priorities "Of what we covered -- which two matter most in the next 90 days?"
Next Steps "Who else needs to be in the next conversation -- and in what order?"

Success in 60-90 Days

"A lot of teams don't have this written down yet -- which is fine. If we fast-forward 60-90 days after a focused pilot, what would you want to be true so you'd say 'this was worth it'?" click to copy

Notes

Objection Quick Reference

If needed
"We already have an internal developer portal" +

What it means: Pain is real -- they invested eng capacity. Portal is likely a visibility layer, not enforceable system of record.

Reframe: "The fact that you built a portal tells me this problem is real. Keep it as the front door. Use Postman as the out-of-the-box management plane underneath -- integrate instead of rebuilding."
"We use Datadog for observability" +

What it means: O11y vendors don't provide dev-test visibility on APIs. Gateway vendors miss the dev side.

Reframe: "Datadog is great for runtime. The gap is usually dev-test visibility -- specs versioned with code, contract validation in CI, catalog linking design to production. That's complementary, not competitive."
"Team/Pro version has everything we need" +

What it means: They see Postman as individual productivity, not platform governance.

Reframe: "The question isn't whether devs can test APIs. It's: do you have visibility into all APIs, can you enforce governance across hundreds of services, and can you catch breaking changes before production?"
"Postman is just a testing tool" +

What it means: No connection to Git or CI. No contract enforcement. No governance layer.

Reframe: "Testing is where most orgs start. The shift is turning testing into enforceable governance -- contracts validated in PR, same workflows in CI, org-wide visibility into API health."
"We already have governance / linting" +

What it means: They equate governance with style checks. May not block behavioral breaking changes.

Reframe: "Linting ensures consistency. The real risk is contract drift that breaks downstream teams. Postman works directly with your Git repo -- specs, tests, and collections live alongside code."
"Developers won't adopt another tool" +

What it means: Fear of friction from past top-down tool failures.

Reframe: "Do you have one tool for the entire API lifecycle? This isn't the dev decision -- this is yours to manage APIs at scale."
