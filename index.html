Frame the Call

0-3 min

Set the tone. You're here to listen, not pitch.

"Thanks for taking the time. I'd love to understand how your team builds and manages APIs today so we can figure out if there's a fit. I'll mostly be asking questions -- sound good?" click to copy

Checklist

  • Confirm how much time they have
  • Set expectation: "I'll be asking questions to understand your world"
  • Confirm attendees and their roles
  • Ask if there's anything specific they want to cover

Notes

Start Broad

3-8 min

Understand who they are, what success looks like, and why APIs matter.

Role and Context

  • "Can you walk me through your role and the teams you work most closely with?"
  • "Are you more hands-on, coordination-focused, or setting strategy?"

Success Metrics

  • "What are the top outcomes your team is measured on this quarter or year?"
  • "When leadership asks for API progress, what metrics do they care about most?"

Strategic Context

  • "What are the priorities or strategic initiatives going on in your company?"
  • "How does API fit into or support your company-wide business goals?"
  • "Is your org doing anything to formalize API collaboration -- a platform initiative, partner program, or developer portal?"

Day-to-Day

  • "What does a typical week look like when it comes to APIs -- where do most hours go?"
  • "What parts of the process feel smooth, and where does it usually slow down?"

Notes

Identify the Chaos Type

8-10 min

Use anchor questions to find the chaos. Select what you hear.

5 Anchor Questions

  • "How do teams discover internal APIs today?"
  • "When someone needs to integrate with another team's API, what does day one look like -- and what usually slows them down?"
  • "When an API ships or changes, how do docs, specs, tests, and what's actually running stay in sync?"
  • "Are humans, agents, or both expected to consume these interfaces?"
  • "What would improve if finding, validating, and operating against those APIs got a lot easier?"

⚡
